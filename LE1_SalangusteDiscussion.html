Cybersecurity Discussion

Types of Cybercrimes are:

  1. DDoS Attacks
  2. Identity Theft
  3. Cyberstalking
  4. Social Engineering
  5. Phishing

Some protective measures for Cybercrimes are:

  1. Using an Anti-Virus software
  2. Not Giving personal informations, unless secure
  3. Being mindful on sites being visited
  4. Keeping an eye on your bank statements
  5. Using strong passwords

Types of Cybercrimes

1. DDos Attacks

These are used to make an online service unavailable and take the network down by overwhelming the site with traffic from a variety of sources.
Large networks of infected devices known as Botnets are created by depositing malware on users’ computers.

2. Identity Theft

This cybercrime occurs when a criminal gains access to a user’s personal information to steal funds, access confidential information, or participate in tax or health insurance fraud.
They can also open an account in your name, use your name to plan a criminal activity and claim government benefits.

3. Cyberstalking

This kind of cybercrime involves online harassment where the user is subjected to a plethora of online messages and emails.
Typically cyberstalkers use social media, websites and search engines to intimidate a user and instill fear.
Usually, the cyberstalker knows their victim and makes the person feel unsafe.

4. Social Engineering

Social engineering involves criminals making direct contact with you usually by phone or email.
Cybercriminals will find out what they can about you on the internet and then attempt to add you as a friend on social accounts.

5. Phishing

This type of attack involves hackers sending malicious email attachments or URLs to users to gain access to their accounts or computer.
Users are tricked into emails claiming they need to change their password or update their billing information, giving criminals access.

Protective Measures

1. Using an Anti-Virus Software

Using anti-virus or a comprehensive internet security solution like Kaspersky Total Security is a smart way to protect your system from attacks.
Anti-virus software allows you to scan, detect and remove threats before they become a problem. Having this protection in place helps to protect your computer and your data.

2. Not Giving personal informations, unless secure

Never give out personal data over the phone or via email unless you are completely sure the line or email is secure.
Make certain that you are speaking to the person you think you are.

3. Being mindful on sites being visited

Keep an eye on the URLs you are clicking on. Do they look legitimate? Avoid clicking on links with unfamiliar or spammy looking URLs.
If your internet security product includes functionality to secure online transactions, ensure it is enabled before carrying out financial transactions online.

4. Keeping an eye on your bank statements

Our tips should help you avoid falling foul of cybercrime.
However, if all else fails, spotting that you have become a victim of cybercrime quickly is important.
Keep an eye on your bank statements and query any unfamiliar transactions with the bank.
The bank can investigate whether they are fraudulent.

5. Using strong passwords

Be sure to use strong passwords that people will not guess and do not record them anywhere.
Or use a reputable password manager to generate strong passwords randomly to make this easier.
